Rain hammered the broken roof. Water splashed across shattered glass and rusted tools.

Two kids stood in the dark, small frames trembling. One gripped a flashlight; its beam wavered with every shake. The other pressed a bloodied rag to his arm, teeth clenched, refusing to cry out.

Somewhere in the shadows, a third figure moved. Silent. Watching.

"We shouldn't be here…" the first whispered.

"You said we stick together. Always," the other snapped, forcing his breath steady.

A loud bang rattled the doors. Both flinched. The flashlight jerked upward, catching dust drifting like ghosts.

The shadow stepped closer. No face. Just presence.

"What if they find us?"

A tense pause.

"They already have."

The door creaked open. White light flooded the room. The kids froze.

And vanished.
